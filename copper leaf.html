Copper leaf

Scientific Name-Acalypha wilkesiana
Genus-Acalypha
Family-Euphorbiaceae
Order-Malpighiales
Class-Magnoliophyta
Kingdom- Plantae
Species-A. wilkesiana
Origin-Copperleaf is native to the islands of the southern Pacific. It has long been cultivated by Polynesian peoples, so its actual homeland is a bit uncertain. The name copperleaf is commonly applied to -Acalypha wilkesiana, a popular shrub of tropical gardens that has red, blotched reddish brown, and pink foliage. It is also widely known as Jacob's coat.
propagation -It is mostly propagated from cuttings. Copperleaf prefers a compost rich soil, so you can add more of compost like vermi-compost or cowdung into this potting mix. Copperleaf plants propagate most easily with high humidity, so enclose the pot in a plastic bag and keep it in a warm, bright spot.
Description-Copperleaf is an evergreen shrub that can grow upto 3m tall and 2m wide. It has a closely arranged crown, with an erect stem and many branches. Both the branches and the leaves are covered in fine hairs. Copperleaf has small flower in racemes or spikes 10 to 20cm long, male and female flowers look different but are borne on the same plant. Copperleaf has large, round palmate like leaves with serrated margin, coppery-bronze to green in color. The foliage can be red, blotched reddish brown and pink.
Use-Best used topically to speed the resolution of slowly healing wounds and ulcers.
Variety-Red copperleaf plant, white copperleaf, Cream, purple, pink, green, foxtail copperleaf, feild copperleaf, streambank copperleaf etc.
