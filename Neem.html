Neem

Scientific Name-Azadirachta indica
Genus-Azadirachta
Family-Meliaceae
Order-Sapindales
Kingdom- Plantae
Species-A. indica
Origin-The neem tree is thought to have originated in the Indian subcontinent and dry areas of south Asia. It is also native to Assam and Burma, and is common in the Centraldry zone and the Siwalik hills. The neem tree has been introduced to parts of Africa, the Caribbean and many countries in south and central America.
propagation -Neem trees can be propagated by seed, cuttings, or root suckers.
Description-The neem tree is a medium-sized, fast-growing, evergreen tree that can reach 15-30m in height. It has a rounded crown, thick furrowed bak, and spreading branches. The leaves are compound, with toothed leaflets that are typically evergreen but may drop during periods of extreme drought. The leaves are up to 40 cm long and have a flexible central midrib with 20-30 paired leaflets, each one 3-8cm long.
Use-Medicinal use, Insect repellent, toothpaste and mouthwash etc
