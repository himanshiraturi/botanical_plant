Rudraksha Tree

Scientific Name- Elaeocarpus angustifolius
Genus- Elaeocarpus
Family- Elaeocarpaceae
Order- Oxalidales
Class- Magnoliopsida
Division- Magnoliophyta
Kingdom- Plantae
Hindi Name- Rudraksha
origin- Himalayas in Nepal, and India east to China and the Philippines and South to Indonesia, Papua New Guinea and Australia.
Description- Tree typically grows to a height of 40m and usually has buttress roots at the trunck. The leaves are about 60-180mm long, 40-60mm wide with wavy serrations on the edges and tapering to a petiole 5-15mm long, but lacking a pulvinus. Old leaves often turn bright red before falling.
Cultivation- Stem cutting, seed sowing.
Uses- Rudraksha is an auspicious bead, used as prayer beads. This spiritual bead is considered a personification of Lord Shiva.Rudraksha is a compound term in sanskrit, which means Lord Shiva's treardeop (split into two words) Rudra (Lord shiva's vedic name) and Aksha teardrop.
