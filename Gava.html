Guava

Scientific Name-Psidium guajava L.
Genus-Psidium
Species-p. guajava
Family-Myrtaceae
Order-Myrtales
Class-Magnoliopsida
Division-Magnoliophyta
Kingdom- Plantae
Origin-In Tropical America, from Mexico to Peru and in the Caribbean.
Description-Guavas have a musky, at times pungent, odor. The pale pink flesh and juice are sweet and mild, sometimes described as a cross between a ripe pear and a strawberry.

Cultivation-Planting is done during the rainy season. June-July is the ideal time for planting the layers and seedling. Pits should be 45*45*45 cm and spaced 6.5*6.5m.
Medicinal Uses-Guava are rich in antioxidants, vitamin C, potassium and fiber.
Variety-Allahabad safeda, Sardar (Lucknow 49), Pant Prabhat, Lalit, Dhareedar, Chittidar, Harijan, Arka Mridula and Khaja (Bengal Safeda).
