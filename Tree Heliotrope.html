Tree Heliotrope

Scientific Name-Heliotropium foertherianum
Genus-Heliotropium
Family-Boraginaceae
Order-Boraginales
Class-Dicotyledons
Kingdom- Plantae
Species-Tree heliotrope
Origin-The heliotrope is a flowering plant native to Bolivia, Colombia and Peru. Indian heliotrope is native to Asia and invasive in parts of the United states. Its distribution just barely includes New England, collections having been made only in Massachusetts.
Description-It is a bushy, evergreen shrub that can grow up to 3.9ft tall and broad. The heliotropehas dark green foliage with prominent veins, and ovate leaves that feel rough. It blooms in clusters with purple, white, blue, or pink flowers. The heliotrope has a vanilla-like scent and all parts of the plants are poisonous, but toxic to humans only if consumed in large quantities. The heliotropeis usually grown as an annual, but may be grown as a perennialin zones 9 and 10. Bedding plants can be allowed to die each fall, and new plants can be purchased or raised from seed each spring. Larger potted or container plants may be overwintered in bright cool.
propagation-Heliotropes can be propagated by stem cuttings in soil anytime during the active growing season (spring and summer). In fact, starting cuttings from an established outdoor plant may be the easiest way to start growing this flowering shrub indoors.
Medicinal Uses-Treating warts, inflammations, and tumors, treating skin problems such as yaws, uticaria, scabies, ulcers, eczema, and impetigo, treating gastric and varicose ulcerations, regulating menstruation etc.
Variety-Heliotropium amplexicaule vahl, Heliotropium arborescens, Nagano, Fragrant delight.
