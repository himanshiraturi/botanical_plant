Royal Palm

Scientific Name- Roystonea regia
Genus - Roystonea
Family - Arecaceae
Order - Arecales
Class - Liliopsida
Division - Magnoliophyta
Species - R. regia
Origin - Mexico, the Caribbean, Florida, and parts of Central America.
Varieties - Florida royal palm, Roystonea oleracea, Cuban royal palm.
Time Of planting - Spring or early summer.

Description - The royal palm is a large majestic palm that is native to south Florida and Cuba. It is considered cold hardy to about 28 degree F or USDA cold Hardiness Zone 10A. This species quickly grows to heights of 50-70ft with a spread of 20-25ft, and has a smooth light gray trunk up to 2ft in diameter.
Medicinal use - The seed is used as a source of oil and for livestock feed.
Cultivation- It is cultivated by seed germination.
