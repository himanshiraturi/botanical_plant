Butterfly bush

Scientific Name-Buddleja davidii
Genus-Buddleja
Family-Scrophulariaceae
Order-Lamiales
Class-Magnoliopsida
Kingdom- Plantae
Species-B. davidii
Origin-Although Butterfly Bush grows easily in our region, it is not native to North America. The Buddleja genus originated in Central China and migrated across Asia and to the Americas, evolving into over 140 species as it spred. B. davidii, the most commonly cultivated species in our region, is a native of China.
Description-The butterfly bush is a fast-growing, woody shrub that can grow up to 15 feet tall in a single season. It has opposite, pale-gray leaves with toothed or wavy margins, and flowers that are grouped in a cluster at the branch tips. The flowers are showy, fragrant, and attract pollinators. They bloom from mid-summer to early fall and can be pink, red, white, blue, or yellow. The butterfly blush is native to tropical and subtropical areas of the world. It has a fibrous root system, instead of a taproot, which means the roots get their nutrients from the upper layers of soil. It requires little work beyond well-drained soil and good sunlight.

propagation-Cutting: cut a branch near a healthy bud that is four to six inches long. Plant the branch in fertile soil with direct sunlight and water regularly until the roots take hold. Division of roots: Carefully dig up mature butterfly bushes and remove excess soil. Then either separate the roots by hand or use a spade shovel to divide the plants.
Medicinal Uses-The butterfly bush may be used as a diuretic and pain relieve pain and inflammation. The leaves can be used to make tea.
Variety-Black knight, Butterfly bush, Blue chip, Purple haze, Pink delight, Prince charming etc.
