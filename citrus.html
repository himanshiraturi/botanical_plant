Citrus

Scientific Name- Citrus spp.
Genus-Citrus
Family-Rutaceae
Order-Sapindales
Class-Dicotyledons
Kingdom- Plantae
Origin- Citrus trees originated in an extensive area covering Asia (from India to the North of China) and Oceania (Queensland, Australia).All citrus species available today came from the south-east foothillsof the Himalayas, specifically the eastern areas of Assam, northern Myanmar and Western Yunnan in China.
Description-Citrus fruits are a group of juicy, flavorful fruits that are known for their high vitamin C content. The term "citrus" refers to a specific group of fruits that belong to the genus citrus,, which includes: Oranges, Lemons, Lime, Grapesfruits, Bitter Orange, Clementine. Citrus fruits are notable for their fragrance, partly due to flavonoids and limonoids (which in turn are terpenes) contained in the rind. The juice contains a high quantity of citric acid and other organic acids giving them their characteristic sharp flavor.

propagation-Generally acid lime is propagation by seeds and sweet oranges by budding. For raising seedlings, freshly extracted seeds should be sown, 2.5cm*7.5cm apart on raised seed beds prepared from well pulverized and heavily manured soil with decayed leaf mold or well-rotted cow dung. The seeda are sown in May-June or September-October and the seedlings will be ready for transplanting after 6 to 8 monthsof sowing. In case of acid lime, when they attaind pencil thickness. Bud material should be procured from the virus free the virus free parent trees.
Medicinal Uses- Citrus fruits have many health benefits including: heart health, skin health, immune system, antioxidants, phytochemicals, low in calories.
Variety- Rangpur Lime, Kagzi Lime, Lemons, Sweet Lime, Khasi Orange, Coorg Orange, Desi, Kinnow Orange, Darjeeling Orange, Mosami, Malta, Malta (blood red), Sathgudi.
