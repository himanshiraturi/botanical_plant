Ficus

Scientific Name-Ficus benjamina
Genus-Ficus
Family-Moraceae
Order-Rosales
Class-Magnoliophyta
Kingdom- Plantae
Origin-The ficus genus of plants is native to tropical areas of East Asia. The genus contains over 800 species of trees, shrubs, and vines. Many species are known as fig trees because they bear fig like-substances.
Description-Ficus is a genus of about 850 species of woody trees, shrubs, vines, epiphytes, and hemiepiphytes in the family Moraceae. They are collectively known as fig trees or figs.Most ficus species are evergreen there are a few deciduous members in nontropical areas.

propagation-To propagate by Stem cutting.
Medicinal Uses-Its latex and some fruits extracts are used by indigenous communities to treat skin disorders, inflammation, piles, vomitng, leprosy, malaria, nose diseases, and cancer besides the use as a general tonic.
Variety-Ficus benjamina Danielle, Ficus benjamina variegata, Golden king etc.
