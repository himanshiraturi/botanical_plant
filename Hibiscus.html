China rose

Scientific Name-Hibiscus rosa-sinensis
Genus-Hibicus
Family-Malvaceae
Order-Malvales
Class-Magnoliopsida
Kingdom- Plantae
Species- rosa-sinensis
Origin-The China rose is thought to have originated in South-Central and South-West China. It is one of the oldest cultivated roses, having been brought from China before 1894. The China rose is a rounded, woody, deciduous shrub in the rose family (Rosaceae). The genus name rosa is latin for rose. The specific epithet indicates its origin. The china rose is native to the Guizhou, Hubei, Sichuan provinces of Southwest China.
Description-The China rose is a species of shrub in the mallow family. It has simple, broad leaves and capsule fruit. The flowers are trumpet shaped and consist of five petals with a variety of colors. The standard flowers are typically medium to dark red, but there are dozens of cultivars that include pink, orange, yellow and white. The China rose is one of the oldest cultivated roses, having been brought from China before 1894. It is native to South and central china.
propagation-A grafting cuktivation method of Chinese rose tree or rose tree. Grafting is done from the end of summer to the beginning of autumn, and the grafting part is as close to the ground as possible. Cutting: china rose can propagate easily from both softwood and hardwood cuttings taken in early summer or late autumn. Rooting hormone is not a necessary step in the cutting and propagating process, but it will increase the chances that your cutting will grow successfully. Seeds: seed propagation often results in variations among the genotypes.
Medicinal Uses-China rose is a flower that has traditionally been used for hair growth and for the treatment of stomach ulcers. It has general antioxidant properties, but is otherwise underresearched. China rose is most often used for gut health.
